About

Mission

Satchitananda Ranch is a sacred space for holding retreats dedicated to raising consciousness. We serve leaders in taking the time they need to build a strong physical, mental, emotional and energetic foundation from which they can support their communities. We focus on uplifting leading entrepreneurs, investors, academics, musicians, artists, athletes and politicians who bring their learnings and well-being to the broader world.

Land

The ranch includes 600 pristine acres of rolling hills, sage brush, ponds, and aspen groves bordering 90k+ acres of untouched national forest. The panoramic views of the Wind River and Wyoming mountains are awe-inspiring and vast. Simply being at the ranch offers profound healing energy reconnecting with nature and the five elements. Guests can mingle with the ranch horses and a variety of wildlife including eagles, deer, antelope, badgers, frogs, and ducks.

Spaces

The property includes luxurious houses, cabins, yoga/meditation spaces, hot tubs, and cold plunges in which visitors can relax and refresh. The ranch also includes a 25k square foot consecrated farm that provides fresh local energized fruits and vegetables to retreat participants to support healthy eating and detoxification.

Team

Dakin Sloss

Founder

Dakin Sloss is an entrepreneur, investor, and philanthropist. He is Founder and General Partner of Prime Movers Lab, the world’s leading partner of breakthrough scientific startups. He has led investments in transformative companies including Tarana, Halo, CarbonCapture, Focused Energy, Gilgamesh, Paradromics, Vaxxinity, Diamond Age, E-Space, Space Perspective, and Boom Supersonic.

Prior to founding Prime Movers Lab, Dakin served as founding CEO of Tachyus and OpenGov. He is also a philanthropist through the Satchitananda Foundation dedicated to delivering food, freedom and flourishing to our global human family. Dakin studied Mathematics, Physics, and Philosophy at Stanford University.

Dakin has been recognized as one of Yahoo Finance’s THE NEXT: 21 people who will have a significant impact on the worlds of finance, business, sports or politics in the year ahead and was featured on CNBC. In 2016, Dakin was recognized as the featured Forbes 30 Under 30 energy entrepreneur. In 2017, he was named as a San Francisco Business Times 40 Under 40 honoree.
